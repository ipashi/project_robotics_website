Niyantrana(The Gesture Control Robot)

“Just a small gesture could change the things around”. Ever imagined things moving at your gestures? Seems hypothetical? Well! Not really! Here is an event where you get to control your robot, not by switches, but by gestures! An important aspect of a successful robotic system is the Human Machine interaction. In the early years the only way to communicate with a robot was to program which required extensive hard work. With the development in science and robotics, gesture based recognition came into life. Gestures originate from any bodily motion or state but commonly originate from the face or hand. Gesture recognition can be considered as a way for computer to understand human body language. The traditional wired buttons controlled robot becomes very bulgy and it also limits the distance the robot goes. The Wireless Hand controlled Robot will function by a wearable hand glove from which the movements of the hand can be used as the input for the movement of the robot.
